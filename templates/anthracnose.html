Anthracnose

What is Anthracnose?

Anthracnose is a group of fungal diseases that cause dark, sunken lesions on leaves, stems, flowers, or fruits. It is particularly common in warm, wet conditions and can severely affect plant health and productivity.

Symptoms

  • Dark, sunken spots on leaves and fruits
  • Leaf curling and premature drop
  • Lesions on stems and flowers
  • Fruit rot in humid conditions

Commonly Affected Plants

  • Mango
  • Beans
  • Tomatoes
  • Cucurbits

Causes

Caused by fungi from the "Colletotrichum" species, it spreads through water splashes, wind, or infected plant material. High humidity and poor air circulation accelerate its spread.

Prevention & Control

  • Use certified disease-free seeds and plants
  • Prune and destroy infected plant parts
  • Apply recommended fungicides early
  • Avoid overhead irrigation
