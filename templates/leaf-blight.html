Leaf Blight

What is Leaf Blight?

Leaf Blight is a common plant disease caused by various fungi and bacteria. It leads to rapid death of leaf tissue, typically beginning at the tips or edges and spreading inward. It severely affects the plant’s ability to photosynthesize.

Symptoms

  • Brown or tan spots starting at leaf edges
  • Irregular lesions that enlarge over time
  • Yellowing and premature leaf drop
  • Dry, scorched appearance on leaves

Commonly Affected Plants

  • Maize (corn)
  • Rice
  • Wheat
  • Tomatoes

Causes

Caused by pathogens such as "Helminthosporium turcicum" in corn, or "Xanthomonas oryzae" in rice. Poor air circulation, prolonged leaf wetness, and high humidity contribute to outbreaks.

Prevention & Control

  • Use resistant crop varieties
  • Crop rotation and field sanitation
  • Avoid overhead watering
  • Apply appropriate fungicides or bactericides
