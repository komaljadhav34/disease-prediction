Clubroot

What is Clubroot?

Clubroot is a soil-borne disease caused by the pathogen "Plasmodiophora brassicae". It affects the roots of cruciferous plants, causing swelling and distortion that hinder water and nutrient uptake.

Symptoms

  • Swollen, deformed roots resembling clubs
  • Stunted growth and wilting in warm weather
  • Yellowing and premature wilting of leaves
  • Plants easily pulled from the soil

Commonly Affected Plants

  • Cabbage
  • Cauliflower
  • Broccoli
  • Brussels sprouts

Causes

Clubroot is caused by a protist pathogen and spreads through infested soil, equipment, or water. It thrives in acidic, moist soils and persists for many years in the soil.

Prevention & Control

  • Use disease-free transplants and rotate crops
  • Raise soil pH by liming to above 7.2
  • Improve drainage and avoid soil compaction
  • Remove and destroy infected plants
