Downy Mildew

What is Downy Mildew?

Downy Mildew is a plant disease caused by water molds (oomycetes) that affect a wide range of crops. It is characterized by yellow to brown leaf spots and a fuzzy, grayish or white growth on the undersides of leaves, especially in moist conditions.

Symptoms

  • Yellow, angular spots on upper leaf surfaces
  • Grayish or white fuzzy mold on undersides of leaves
  • Leaf curling and browning
  • Stunted plant growth

Commonly Affected Plants

  • Grapevines
  • Cucumbers and melons
  • Spinach
  • Basil

Causes

Caused by oomycetes such as "Plasmopara viticola" or "Peronospora" species. Downy mildew thrives in cool, wet, and humid conditions and spreads rapidly through water splashes and wind.

Prevention & Control

  • Plant resistant varieties
  • Water early in the day to allow leaves to dry
  • Improve air circulation with proper spacing
  • Use appropriate fungicides during wet seasons
