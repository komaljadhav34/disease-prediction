Wilt

What is Wilt?

Wilt is a plant disease that causes plants to lose rigidity and droop due to the blockage of water transport in the vascular system. It is commonly caused by fungi, bacteria, or nematodes that invade plant roots or stems.

Symptoms

  • Sudden wilting of leaves during the day
  • Yellowing of lower leaves
  • Stunted growth and leaf drop
  • Darkening or discoloration of vascular tissue

Commonly Affected Plants

  • Tomatoes
  • Bananas
  • Cotton
  • Peas and beans

Causes

Caused by soil-borne pathogens like "Fusarium oxysporum", "Verticillium spp.",or "Ralstonia solanacearum". These pathogens attack the plant’s vascular system, preventing water from reaching the leaves.

Prevention & Control

  • Use resistant plant varieties
  • Practice crop rotation
  • Remove and destroy infected plants
  • Improve soil drainage and sterilize soil if possible
