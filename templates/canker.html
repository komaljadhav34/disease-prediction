Canker

What is Canker?

Canker is a plant disease characterized by sunken, dead areas on stems, branches, or trunks. It is caused by bacterial or fungal pathogens and can lead to dieback and reduced productivity in plants.

Symptoms

  • Sunken, dark lesions on stems and bark
  • Cracking or oozing of sap from infected areas
  • Wilting and dieback of branches
  • Discoloration or girdling of stems

Commonly Affected Plants

  • Citrus
  • Apple
  • Stone fruits (peach, plum)
  • Willows and poplars

Causes

Canker is caused by fungi (like "Botryosphaeria", "Nectria") or bacteria ("Xanthomonas", "Pseudomonas"). It spreads through wounds and pruning cuts, especially in humid or wet conditions.

Prevention & Control

  • Prune infected parts well below the affected area
  • Disinfect tools between cuts
  • Avoid wounding trees unnecessarily
  • Use resistant plant varieties when available
