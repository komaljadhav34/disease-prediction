Sooty Mold

What is Sooty Mold?

Sooty mold is a fungal growth that appears as a black, soot-like coating on leaves, stems, and fruits. It does not infect plants directly but grows on the sugary honeydew excreted by pests like aphids, whiteflies, and scale insects.

Symptoms

  • Black, powdery coating on leaves and stems
  • Reduced photosynthesis and plant vigor
  • Sticky leaves due to honeydew buildup
  • Fruits and vegetables may appear dirty or blemished

Commonly Affected Plants

  • Citrus
  • Hibiscus
  • Tomatoes
  • Gardenias

Causes

Sooty mold is caused by various fungi that thrive on honeydew secreted by sap-sucking insects. The presence of these pests is the primary cause of the mold’s development.

Prevention & Control

  • Control aphids, whiteflies, and scale insects promptly
  • Wash leaves with water to remove mold and honeydew
  • Encourage beneficial insects like ladybugs
  • Use insecticidal soap or neem oil as needed
