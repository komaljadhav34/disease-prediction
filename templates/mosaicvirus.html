Mosaic Virus

What is Mosaic Virus?

Mosaic Virus is a viral disease that affects many plants, causing mottled light and dark green or yellow mosaic patterns on leaves. It reduces photosynthesis, weakens the plant, and can severely reduce crop yield.

Symptoms

  • Patchy light and dark green or yellow leaf patterns
  • Leaf curling, distortion, or wrinkling
  • Stunted growth and reduced yield
  • Fruit discoloration or deformity

Commonly Affected Plants

  • Tomatoes
  • Tobacco
  • Cucumbers
  • Peppers

Causes

Caused by viruses like *Tobacco Mosaic Virus (TMV)* and *Cucumber Mosaic Virus (CMV)*. It spreads through infected tools, hands, insects (like aphids), and contact with infected plants.

Prevention & Control

  • Use virus-free seeds and plants
  • Control insect vectors like aphids
  • Disinfect tools regularly
  • Remove and destroy infected plants immediately
