Powdery Mildew

What is Powdery Mildew?

Powdery Mildew is a fungal disease that affects a wide variety of plants. It appears as white or grayish powder-like spots, primarily on the upper surfaces of leaves. While it rarely kills the plant, it can weaken it significantly and reduce yields.

Symptoms

  • White, powdery fungal growth on leaves, stems, and buds
  • Distorted or curled leaves
  • Yellowing of infected leaves
  • Premature leaf drop

Commonly Affected Plants

  • Squash and cucumbers
  • Roses
  • Grapes
  • Peas

Causes

Caused by various species of fungi like "Erysiphe", "Podosphaera", and "Sphaerotheca". It thrives in warm, dry climates with high humidity at night and poor air circulation.

Prevention & Control

  • Grow resistant plant varieties
  • Ensure good air circulation by proper spacing
  • Prune and remove infected plant parts
  • Apply sulfur or potassium bicarbonate-based fungicides
